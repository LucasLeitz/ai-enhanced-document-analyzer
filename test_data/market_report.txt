The company released its quarterly earnings report on Tuesday. Revenue increased by 18 percent year over year, driven largely by subscription growth and expansion into international markets. Operating expenses also rose due to higher hiring costs and investments in cloud infrastructure.

Leadership stated that customer retention remains strong and annual recurring revenue exceeded internal expectations. However, executives warned that macroeconomic uncertainty may reduce enterprise spending during the second half of the year.

The company plans to continue investing in artificial intelligence tools and customer analytics products to improve long-term competitiveness.
