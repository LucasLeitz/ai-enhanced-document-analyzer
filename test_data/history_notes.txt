The Roman Empire reached its greatest territorial extent under Emperor Trajan in the second century AD. Its road systems, military organization, and legal institutions helped unify a vast region stretching across Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

Over time, political instability, economic pressure, military overexpansion, and repeated invasions weakened imperial control. In 476 AD, the Western Roman Empire formally collapsed, although the Eastern Roman Empire continued for nearly one thousand more years as the Byzantine Empire.

Roman influence remains visible today in architecture, law, language, and systems of government.
